## R3CAP

<p><b>SLAM, Gaussian Splatting | Python, NeRF Studio, Babylon.js, WebXR</b></p>

### Overview

<p style="margin-top:-10px;">R3CAP is an open-source project that blends SLAM, digital twins, and collaborative 3D scene editing. It goes beyond traditional 3D reconstruction to create an accessible and practical system for real-world applications in disaster response, medical rehabilitation, and collaborative design.</p>

### Key Features

<p style="margin-top:-10px;">
<ul style="margin-left:-1em;">
<li><b>Progressive 3D Reconstruction</b>: Real-time, accurate reconstruction of physical spaces with immediate visual feedback.</li>
<li><b>Collaborative Editing</b>: Multi-user environment for simultaneous editing and interaction in shared 3D scenes.</li>
<li><b>Cross-Platform</b>: Web-based interface, accessible via standard browsers on desktop, tablet, and VR devices.</li>
</ul>
</p>

### Technologies Used

<p style="margin-top:-10px;">
<ul style="margin-left:-1em;">
<li><b>Front-end</b>: Babylon.js, WebXR, Swift</li>
<li><b>Back-end</b>: Python, SLAM, Gaussian Splatting, MongoDB</li></ul>
</p>

### Applications

<p style="margin-top:-10px;">
<ul style="margin-left:-1em;">
<li><b>Medical Rehabilitation</b>: Virtual environments for physical therapy and recovery.</li>
<li><b>Disaster Response</b>: Rapid 3D mapping of affected areas for emergency planning.</li>
<li><b>Architectural Design</b>: Collaborative design and visualization of architectural projects.</li>
<li><b>Industrial Training</b>: Virtual training environments for complex industrial procedures.</li></ul>
</p>

### Research Impact

<p style="margin-top:-10px;">
R3CAP advances spatial computing by combining SLAM, collaborative 3D editing, and accessible web-based tools. Its open-source framework enables researchers and developers to adapt the system across diverse domains, from healthcare to emergency response.
</p>

<p style="margin-top:-6px;">
Currently, the platform is being piloted with <a href="https://www.ttsh.com.sg/Pages/default.aspx">Tan Tock Seng Hospital</a> (TTSH), Singapore, to support virtual rehabilitation in clinical settings, demonstrating its potential for real-world applications.
</p>
